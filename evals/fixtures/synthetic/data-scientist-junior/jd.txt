Junior Data Scientist
Borealis Labs — Hybrid (Toronto, ON)

About Borealis
Borealis Labs is a climate-analytics startup. We sell forecasting tools to municipal governments and utilities so they can plan infrastructure investments around shifting climate baselines. We are a 24-person company, post-seed, building out the data science team from two to four.

The role
You'll join the data science team and own one of our core forecasting modules. This is a ground-floor seat — you'll work directly with the team lead, contribute to model design, and help shape how we evaluate forecast quality.

What you'll do
- Build and improve regression and time-series models for municipal climate-impact forecasts.
- Wrangle messy public datasets (weather stations, satellite-derived data, census records) into modeling-ready form.
- Communicate model behavior and limitations to non-technical clients via clear writing and a few visualizations.
- Pair with senior data scientists on code review; learn the team's evaluation discipline.

What we're looking for
- 1–2 years of professional data work (DS, analytics, research, comparable academic).
- Working competence in Python and SQL.
- Hands-on with at least one of: scikit-learn, statsmodels, pandas, NumPy.
- Strong written communication — you can explain a model's assumptions in plain English.
- Curiosity about climate / public-sector use cases.

Nice to have
- Experience with time-series forecasting (ARIMA, Prophet, gradient-boosted trees).
- Geospatial data familiarity (GeoPandas, PostGIS).
- Open-source contributions or public notebooks.

Compensation
CAD $90–$110K base, hybrid Toronto (2 days/week in office). Junior-friendly culture: explicit mentorship hours, pair programming, code-review training.
